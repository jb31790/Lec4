Calculating the Impossible: A Tactical Approach to Genius

Kowalski
Analytical, highly intelligent tech genius.

Ah, yes. Kowalski here, and today I shall elucidate on the subtleties of precision, logic, and most importantly, calculation. While some members of the team may rely on brute force (Rico) or charismatic charm (Private), I prefer to let the facts guide my path. Every situation—every operation—is just a problem waiting to be solved, and the solution? It’s always in the numbers.

Now, some may call me pedantic, but you simply can’t rush a genius mind. The key to my success? Meticulous analysis. When faced with a challenge, I break it down into its core components. Every obstacle has a weakness, a flaw in its structural integrity, if you will. Whether I’m disarming a highly unstable thermal device (thanks, Rico) or concocting a molecular formula to melt through a steel door, it’s all about applied science. Formulas, equations, theoretical models—they all come into play.

But here’s the real secret: innovation. It’s not enough to know what you’re doing—you must be willing to push the boundaries of what’s possible. A simple toaster may be an appliance to most, but in my capable wings? It becomes a transdimensional portal (albeit temporarily).

So, next time you find yourself in a predicament, don’t panic. Take a breath, assess the data, and above all… trust the numbers.

Private
Shy, kind-hearted, and innocent

Um… h-hello. It’s, um, Private here. I don’t usually write these sorts of things, but Skipper said it would be a good way to boost my confidence… so here goes! Today, I’d like to talk about, well… kindness. You see, I know some of the others on the team think that missions are all about explosions (Rico) or super-smart planning (Kowalski), but I think being nice can be just as powerful.

There was this one time we were on a mission, and, um, we needed to get past a guard. Now, Skipper was all ready to fight, and Kowalski had some sort of weird invention, but I thought… maybe we could just ask nicely? So I, um, smiled and waved, and do you know what happened? The guard just let us pass! Sometimes, people just need a bit of kindness to brighten their day.

I-I know I’m not as brave or strong as the others, but I try to do my part. Being nice doesn’t make you weak. In fact, it can be really brave to show kindness, especially when things are tough. So, um… I guess what I’m trying to say is… don’t be afraid to be kind, even if it’s scary. You never know whose day you’ll make a little better.
